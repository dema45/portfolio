Reservation System

Reservation System is a user-friendly platform where users can effortlessly check flight availability, select routes, and make secure reservations for a variety of services, including scenic flights, medical transport, and private charters—via both helicopter and airplane options

  • Category: Website
  • Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Duration: March 2025 - May 2025
  • Tools: Figma,HTML/CSS

Problem

Bhutan currently lacks a streamlined, user-friendly digital platform for booking both helicopter and airplane services. Users face challenges in checking real-time flight availability, customizing routes, and making secure reservations. The absence of an intuitive interface and efficient booking flow hinders accessibility, particularly for tourists and users with urgent needs such as medical transport.

Project Goal

To design and develop a responsive and user-centric flight reservation system that simplifies the booking process for both helicopter and airplane services. The platform will enable users to check flight availability, customize routes, and make secure reservations for services such as scenic tours, medical evacuations, and private charters. It will ensure seamless navigation, real-time updates, and a personalized experience for both local and international users.

Role

As the lead UX designer for this project, I was responsible for creating a user-friendly and efficient booking system. My focus was on understanding the needs of both local and international users to design an intuitive interface that streamlined reservations and provided real-time updates for both helicopter and airplane bookings.

Responsibilities

I conducted thorough user research to identify booking preferences across different flight types, developed wireframes and interactive prototypes to improve the booking flow, and led usability testing to gather feedback. I designed visually appealing and accessible interfaces, collaborated closely with developers to ensure accurate implementation, and continuously refined the design based on user input. I also documented design guidelines to maintain consistency and enhance the overall user experience.

Pain Points

Complex and Time-Consuming Booking Process

Many users find the traditional flight reservation process in Bhutan overly complicated and lengthy, leading to frustration and a higher likelihood of booking abandonment.

Cumbersome User Interface

Existing interfaces often involve too many steps and lack intuitiveness, making navigation difficult—especially for first-time or less tech-savvy users.

Lack of Real-Time Availability Updates

Users face challenges in accessing accurate, up-to-date availability for both helicopter and airplane flights, particularly for last-minute bookings, which can result in missed opportunities and added stress.

Unclear Pricing and Hidden Fees

Ambiguities in pricing structures and the presence of hidden costs erode user trust and lead to dissatisfaction, especially when expectations are not met at checkout

Usability Findings

In Round 1, users reported that the interface had too many icons and the items appeared cluttered and disorganized. After revisions, Round 2 revealed that users found the page color unappealing and needed a more intuitive and straightforward payment process. These insights were crucial for improving the design to better align with user expectations and usability standards

Mockups

Usability testing provided valuable insights into how actual users engaged with my design, allowing me to pinpoint areas for improvement and enhance the overall user experience. As a result of the findings, I made refinements to the Login page, including updates to the fonts, color scheme, and icon usage.

High Fidelity Prototype

Developed a high-fidelity prototype for the Reservation System by incorporating insights and refinements gathered from each preceding design phase, including user research, wireframing, and usability testing.


Takeaway

Working on this project enhanced my skills in conducting usability studies and applying user feedback to refine digital interfaces. I learned the importance of visual hierarchy, consistent iconography, and user-friendly navigation. Iterating on the design based on real user input helped me better understand how thoughtful adjustments—like color choices and streamlined payment flows—can significantly improve the overall user experience

Next Step

As the next step, I plan to conduct further testing with a broader user base to validate the latest changes and uncover any remaining usability issues. I also aim to enhance the accessibility of the platform by introducing features like keyboard navigation and contrast adjustments. Additionally, I will explore integrating a help or tutorial feature to support new users through the navigation and payment processes.
