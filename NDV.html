NDV:National Digital Voting System

A National Digital Voting Website using blockchain to ensure a secure, transparent, and tamper-proof election process by enabling verifiable and immutable vote recording while maintaining voter anonymity

  • Category: Web design
  • Role: UI/UX Designer
  • Duration: March 2025 - May 2025
  • Tools: Figma,HTML/CSS

Problem

Traditional voting systems face security threats, voter fraud, and a lack of transparency. A National Digital Voting Website using blockchain can ensure a secure, transparent, and tamper-proof election process by enabling verifiable and immutable vote recording while maintaining voter anonymity.

Project Goal

To provide a new digital voting system that leverages blockchain technology to enhance election security, increase voter participation, and improve transparency.

Role

As the Lead UX Designer for the National Digital Voting (NDV) System, I was responsible for designing a secure, intuitive, and accessible voting experience for all Bhutanese citizens, including those in remote regions and abroad. My role involved leading the user experience strategy, translating complex blockchain-based processes into a simple and user-friendly interface, and ensuring that voters of all technical backgrounds could confidently navigate the platform

Responsibilities

My responsibilities included conducting user research to understand voter needs and pain points, designing wireframes and prototypes that emphasized clarity and trust, and optimizing the platform for both desktop and mobile accessibility. I worked closely with the development team to integrate blockchain-specific features like voter authentication, encrypted vote submission, and real-time vote verification. I also focused on ensuring the platform met high standards of usability, security, and performance, contributing to a transparent and inclusive digital voting process.

Pain Points

Long wait times at polling stations

Implement an online voting system that allows voters to cast their ballots digitally from any location, reducing physical queues.

Risk of voter fraud and manipulation

Utilize blockchain technology to create a secure, immutable, and tamper-proof voting record, ensuring election integrity.

Lack of transparency in vote counting

Enable real-time vote tracking with a decentralized ledger that allows all the voters to verify vote counts independently.

Difficulty in accessing voting facilities for remote or disabled voters

Provide a mobile-friendly digital voting platform, ensuring accessibility for all eligible voters, including those with disabilities

Paper ballot inefficiencies and environmental impact

Transition to a fully digital voting system to eliminate paper waste and reduce the carbon footprint of elections.

High costs associated with traditional elections

Reduce operational expenses by minimizing the need for physical polling stations, printed ballots, and manual vote counting.

Usability Findings

To evaluate the effectiveness and user experience of the National Digital Voting (NDV) platform, I conducted a moderated usability study with six participants aged 19–45, including students, working professionals, and a senior citizen. The goal was to assess how users interact with key voting features such as voter registration, ID verification, secure voting, live result viewing, and system logout, while identifying usability pain points that may hinder voter confidence and participation.

Research Objectives

  • How easily can users register and verify their identity on the NDV platform?
  • Are users confident in the vote-casting process, and do they trust its security?
  • Do users find it easy to access and understand real-time election results?
  • What usability barriers exist for first-time or less tech-savvy voters?
  • Does the platform support a seamless end-to-end digital voting experience?

    • Research Objectives

  • Time on Task: Duration taken to complete tasks like registering, voting, and viewing results.
  • User Error Rates: Instances of navigation confusion, failed submissions, or missed confirmations.
  • Task Completion Rate: Percentage of users who successfully completed the full voting flow.
  • User Satisfaction: Post-session ratings on trust, clarity, and ease of use.
  • Accessibility & Inclusiveness: Observed friction points for users of varying tech literacy levels.

    • Participant Feedback & Insights

    During the usability study of the National Digital Voting (NDV) platform, participants provided a range of feedback that highlighted both strengths and areas for improvement. Participant 1 (Female, 21) mentioned that it took her a while to find the sign-up button, indicating that its placement was not intuitive and created an early obstacle. Participant 2 (Male, 33) found the ID upload process smooth but expressed concern about the lack of confirmation, emphasizing the need for status messages after submitting sensitive documents. Similarly, Participant 6 (Male, 39) echoed this sentiment, saying he was left wondering what happened next after uploading his ID, which pointed to a lack of real-time feedback during verification. Participant 3 (Female, 25) felt confident about the security of the voting process but wished for a final review screen before submission, suggesting a need for a confirmation step to reassure users. Participant 4 (Male, 45) appreciated the real-time election results but found the numerical data hard to interpret and recommended the use of visual aids like graphs for better clarity. On a positive note, Participant 5 (Female, 19) shared that logging out was smooth, quick, and easy, highlighting a well-designed and intuitive logout experience. Collectively, the feedback reveals valuable insights for enhancing usability, user confidence, and overall clarity across the voting journey.

    Synthesized Insights

    Unclear Entry Point

    Issue: Some users struggled to locate the "Sign Up" button.
    Recommendation: Make the registration call-to-action more prominent and visually distinct.

    Missing Feedback on Sensitive Actions

    Issue: Users felt uneasy after uploading ID without confirmation or status updates.
    Recommendation: Add real-time status indicators and confirmation messages for critical submissions.

    Lack of Vote Finalization Reassurance

    Issue: Several participants wanted a review screen before finalizing their vote.
    Recommendation: Add a summary page that allows users to double-check selections before submission.

    Data Visualization Needs

    Issue: Election results were informative but lacked visual clarity.
    Recommendation: Implement visual aids like bar charts or pie graphs to make live data more digestible.

    Smooth Logout Experience

    Strength: All users found logging out straightforward and problem-free.
    Recommendation: Retain this flow as-is, while ensuring session security is maintained.

    Takeaway

    Working on the NDV project significantly strengthened my understanding of designing for trust, accessibility, and clarity in digital government services. Through user research, wireframing, and usability testing, I learned the critical importance of real-time feedback, intuitive navigation, and clear data presentation—especially in high-stakes environments like voting. This experience deepened my skills in translating user concerns into practical design solutions that promote transparency, ease of use, and voter confidence

    Next Step

    Moving forward, I plan to enhance the NDV platform by integrating real-time ID verification status updates and a vote review screen to reassure users before submission. I also aim to improve the visualization of live election results through interactive graphs and charts. To increase inclusivity, I intend to prioritize accessibility features such as screen reader compatibility and simplified navigation for users with varying levels of digital literacy. Additionally, I will explore multilingual support to better serve diverse voter populations.
